A great prompt is a reusable asset. The prompts below are tested, specific, and ready to customize for your business. Replace the bracketed placeholders with your details and run them directly in ChatGPT, Claude, or any other AI tool.

## Marketing Copy Prompts

**1. Landing page headline variations**
"Write 10 headline variations for a landing page selling [product]. The target customer is [describe them]. Include headlines that lead with a benefit, a problem, a specific result, and a question. Keep each under 12 words."

**2. Email subject lines**
"Generate 15 email subject lines for a campaign promoting [offer]. The audience already knows our brand. Mix curiosity, urgency, benefit-driven, and number-based formats. Avoid spam trigger words."

**3. Value proposition**
"Write a one-sentence value proposition for [business]. It should answer: what we do, who we do it for, and what makes us different. Give me 5 options."

**4. Product description**
"Write a product description for [product]. Include: what it is, what problem it solves, three key features explained as benefits, and a single clear call to action. Tone: [your tone]."

**5. About page bio**
"Write an about page for [name/business]. Focus on what drove them to start the business, who they serve, and one surprising detail that builds connection. Keep it under 200 words."

**6. Ad copy (3 versions)**
"Write three Facebook/Instagram ad copy variations for [product]. Version 1: problem-aware audience. Version 2: solution-aware audience. Version 3: retargeting audience who visited but didn't buy. Include headline, body copy, and CTA for each."

**7. Testimonial request email**
"Write an email asking [customer type] to leave a review/testimonial. Make it easy by giving them three questions to answer: What was your situation before? What result did you get? Who would you recommend this to?"

**8. Objection handler**
"The most common objection to [product] is [objection]. Write three ways to address this objection in copy — one that reframes it, one that uses social proof, and one that offers a guarantee or risk reversal."

**9. Upsell offer**
"Write a post-purchase upsell message for customers who just bought [product]. Offer [upsell product]. The tone should feel like a helpful suggestion, not a hard sell."

**10. Referral program message**
"Write a referral program invitation email for existing customers of [business]. Explain what they get for referring and make it feel like sharing a good thing with friends, not earning a commission."

## Customer Service Prompts

**11. Response to negative review**
"Write a professional response to this negative review: [paste review]. Acknowledge the frustration, apologize where appropriate, offer a resolution, and invite them to continue the conversation privately."

**12. FAQ document**
"Create an FAQ document for [business/product] with 10 questions and answers. Cover: how it works, who it's for, pricing, how to get started, refund policy, support options, and common concerns."

**13. Complaint resolution email**
"Write an email resolving a customer complaint about [issue]. Include: acknowledgment, apology, explanation of what happened, what we're doing to fix it, and a goodwill gesture."

**14. Onboarding welcome email**
"Write a welcome email for new customers of [product/service]. Include: a warm welcome, what to do first, where to get help, and one quick win they can get in the first 24 hours."

**15. Check-in email for inactive customers**
"Write a check-in email for customers who haven't engaged in 60 days. Offer value first (tip, resource, or update), then gently ask if they need help or have questions. No hard sell."

## Business Planning Prompts

**16. SWOT analysis**
"Conduct a SWOT analysis for [business description]. Be specific and honest. For weaknesses and threats, also suggest one mitigation strategy for each."

**17. 90-day goals**
"Help me create 90-day goals for [business]. My current situation: [describe]. My 12-month goal: [describe]. Break the 90-day period into three 30-day sprints with specific, measurable targets."

**18. Ideal customer profile**
"Create a detailed ideal customer profile for [business]. Include: demographics, psychographics, main goals, biggest fears, how they find solutions, what they've tried before, and what makes them buy."

**19. Competitive analysis**
"Analyze the competitive landscape for [business type]. Identify: 3-5 main competitors, their positioning, their apparent strengths and weaknesses, gaps in the market, and where I could differentiate."

**20. Pricing strategy**
"I'm pricing [product/service] for [target market]. My costs are [describe]. Competitors charge [range]. Suggest three pricing models — value-based, tiered, and premium — with rationale for each."

## Content Creation Prompts

**21. Blog post outline**
"Create a detailed outline for a blog post titled [title]. Target keyword: [keyword]. Include: intro hook, 5-7 H2 sections with bullet points under each, a conclusion with CTA. Audience: [describe]."

**22. Social media series**
"Create a 5-post social media series on [topic] for [business]. Each post should standalone but connect thematically. Include caption, visual description, and suggested hashtags."

**23. Newsletter**
"Write a weekly newsletter for [business] covering: [topic]. Format: short intro, main insight (3-4 paragraphs), one actionable tip, one resource recommendation, and a sign-off. Tone: [describe]."

**24. Video script**
"Write a 3-minute YouTube/video script about [topic]. Structure: hook (first 10 seconds), problem setup, solution walk-through, proof or example, CTA. Keep sentences short for easy delivery."

**25. Case study**
"Write a customer case study for [business]. The customer: [describe]. The problem they had: [describe]. The solution we provided: [describe]. The results: [describe]. Format as a narrative, not a list."

## Productivity and Operations Prompts

**26. Meeting agenda**
"Create a 60-minute meeting agenda for a team planning session about [topic]. Include: objectives, time blocks for each section, pre-meeting prep tasks, and desired outcomes."

**27. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)**
"Write a step-by-step SOP for [process]. Include: purpose, who is responsible, required tools/access, each step with sub-steps, common mistakes to avoid, and how to measure success."

**28. Job posting**
"Write a job posting for [role] at [company type]. Include: role overview, key responsibilities, requirements, nice-to-haves, what makes this role unique, and how to apply. Tone should attract [type of candidate]."

**29. Decision framework**
"I'm deciding between [option A] and [option B]. Help me think through this with a structured framework. Consider: impact, effort, risk, reversibility, and alignment with [my goal]."

**30. Project retrospective**
"Facilitate a written retrospective for [project]. Questions to address: What went well? What didn't go as planned? What would we do differently? What should we start, stop, or continue?"

## Sales Prompts

**31–40. Sales conversation scripts, objection responses, follow-up sequences, proposal templates, and more** — these follow the same pattern: specific situation, specific role, specific desired outcome. The more context you give, the more useful the output.

## Getting Better Results

Every prompt improves with three additions: context about your business, a description of your audience, and the tone you want. A prompt that takes 30 seconds to customize produces output that takes 30 minutes less to edit.

Build a personal prompt library. When you write a prompt that produces great output, save it. Add notes about what worked. Over time, your prompt library becomes a competitive asset that compounds.
